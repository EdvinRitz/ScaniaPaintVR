# How to use Scania VR

A short player guide for **ScaniaPaintVR** — put on the headset, grab the spray bottle, and paint the truck however you like.

## What you'll see when you start

- A Scania truck in front of you, ready to be painted.
- A **spray bottle** within arm's reach.
- A **color wheel** nearby — your palette for picking what color to spray.
- One or more **teleport spots** on the floor around the truck.

## Moving around the truck

You move using **teleportation** — the standard VR way of getting around without making yourself motion-sick.

1. **Push the thumbstick forward** on a controller (the one you're not using to spray). A curved ray appears from your hand.
2. **Aim the ray** at a spot on the floor near the truck. A circle/marker shows where you'll land.
3. **Release the thumbstick** to teleport to that spot.
4. Need to face a different way? **Flick the thumbstick left or right** to snap-turn in 45° steps.

You can also just **walk and lean** in your room-scale space — leaning in close lets you paint fine details without teleporting.

## Picking up the spray bottle

1. Reach toward the spray bottle with either hand.
2. **Squeeze the grip button** on that controller to grab it.
3. To swap hands, reach over with the other controller and grip — the bottle moves to that hand.
4. **Release the grip** to drop the bottle back into the world.

Only the hand currently holding the bottle can spray.

## Spraying paint

With the bottle in hand:

1. **Point the nozzle** at the truck. A small reticle appears on the surface where your spray will land.
2. **Hold the trigger** on the same hand to start spraying. You'll hear a continuous spray sound.
3. **Sweep the bottle** across the panel to lay down a stroke. Move smoothly for a clean line.
4. **Release the trigger** to stop.

How distance affects your stroke:

- **Up close** (around an arm's length) → small, focused spray for details and edges.
- **Farther back** (a couple of meters) → wide, soft spray for big surfaces.
- **Too far** (beyond ~3 meters) → no paint reaches the truck. Step in or teleport closer.

If the reticle disappears, you're aiming off the truck or out of range — just re-aim.

## Choosing a color

Colors come from the **color wheel** in the scene:

1. With your **free hand** (the one *not* holding the bottle), point the controller at the color wheel — a thin ray comes from it.
2. **Move the ray over the wheel** until it sits on the color you want.
3. **Pull the trigger** on that hand to confirm.
4. The spray bottle's body changes to your new color, and the next time you spray, that's what comes out.

You can change color as often as you like — pick, spray, pick a new color, spray again. Layer colors freely; new spray paints over what's already there.

## Tips for a good paint job

- **Keep the nozzle roughly perpendicular** to the surface — angled spray smears.
- **Move at a steady speed.** Too fast leaves gaps; too slow builds up heavy patches.
- **Start far, finish close.** Lay down broad base coats from a couple of meters, then teleport in for highlights and detail work.
- **Walk around the truck** (teleport to the other side) to reach panels you can't see — don't try to lean through it.
- **Made a mistake?** Just paint over it. Pick the right color and spray again — fresh paint covers old paint.

## Quick controls reference

| Action | Control |
|---|---|
| Teleport | Push thumbstick forward → aim → release |
| Snap turn | Flick thumbstick left or right |
| Grab / drop spray bottle | Grip button on either hand |
| Spray paint | Trigger on the hand holding the bottle |
| Aim the spray | Point the bottle's nozzle |
| Open color picker ray | Point your free hand at the color wheel |
| Pick a color | Trigger on the free hand while pointing at the wheel |
